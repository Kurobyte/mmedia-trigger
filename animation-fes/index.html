Animation Festivals

Trigger Studio has participated in some animation festivals like Anime Mirai(now called Anime Tamago, or Japan Anima(tor)'s Expo.

Anime Mirai(Anime Tamago)

The Young Animator Training Project is an annual project launched in 2010, and funded by the Japanese government's Agency for Cultural Affairs in order to support training animators. The project culminates in a series of anime shorts produced by various animation studios each year. The project has changed its name from Project A in 2011 to Anime Mirai from 2012 to 2015 and now Anime Tamago.

Trigger studio participated in the Anime Mirai 2012 with his first big project. Little Witch Academia, which was a great succes for the studio beacause it had a second film and plans for a future TV Serie(planned to be aired in January 2017).

Japan Anima(tor)'s Expo

The short film series "Japan Anima(tor)'s Expo"(Nihon Animator Mihonichi) presented by Studio Khara and Dwango. Original projects, spin-off projects, promotional films, Music PV, and VJ Films, etc... Various omnibus animations produced with love and energy, regardless of any genres. The digital distribution anime series bring opportunities for project development, R&D, human resources development, and free production under limited period and budget, to explore the possibilities of future film production.

Trigger Studio animated 3 short movies for Animator Expo, GRIDMAN: boys invent great hero, PP33: Power Plant No.33 and SEX and VIOLENCE with MACHSPEED.
